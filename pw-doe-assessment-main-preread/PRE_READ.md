# Director of Engineering Assessment - Pre-Read

**Role:** Director of Engineering
**Assessment format:** Pre-read (this document + `/brief/`) followed by a 4-hour timed build window

---

## What this is

You're interviewing for Director of Engineering at PantryPal, an early-stage B2C startup building an AI-powered cooking assistant. The previous DoE left three weeks ago.

This assessment simulates your first week. In `/brief/` you'll find seven artifacts from the people you'd actually be working with:

- An email from the Head of Product
- A voice-memo transcript from the CEO
- A message from the Head of Customer Experience
- An email from outside legal counsel
- A team handoff document from the outgoing Director of Engineering
- A note from the CEO about board context and targets
- A summary of a recent production incident

Read them carefully. These are the kinds of inputs you'd encounter in your first week: incomplete, sometimes contradictory, with real constraints buried in asides and real team dynamics underneath the surface.

## What happens next

When your build window opens, you'll receive the full assessment instructions with specific deliverables and technical requirements. The 4-hour clock starts at that point.

You don't need to prepare anything in advance. Just read, think, and form your own view of the situation. The build-day instructions will tell you exactly what to produce.

## A note on the brief

Do not assume every stated requirement belongs in your build, and do not assume every omission is unimportant. Reading these artifacts carefully is part of the work.
