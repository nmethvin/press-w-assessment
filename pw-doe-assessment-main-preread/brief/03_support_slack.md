hey! welcome to the chaos. priya said you're starting this week, wanted to dump some stuff from the trenches in case it's useful. I run support + I've been in the beta tester interviews so I have Opinions

in no particular order:

- ok the cookware thing. this is THE problem. our current approach is we assume everyone has the same basic kit of like 8 items, and recipes get matched against that. it does not work. I spent a week doing user interviews and I can tell you that maybe 20% of our users actually map to that list. a bunch of people have way more - instant pots, air fryers, stand mixers, sous vide, the whole thing. and a surprising number have less - I had one user who cooks everything in a single pan on a hot plate. every interview, someone brought up cookware they own that we don't account for, or asked for a recipe that required something they don't have. this is the #1 cause of churn in beta. whatever you do here, it needs to actually reflect what the user has, not what we guessed.

- related: when the bot DOES catch that they can't make something, users still get mad if it just says "you can't make this." they want it to suggest a workaround or a different recipe. "you can't do this, but you CAN do this similar thing" is the move. just refusing is almost as bad as suggesting the wrong thing.

- a lot of our older users (we have more of them than you'd think, like 50s/60s demo is real for us) ask it stuff like "is this safe to eat" when they describe leftovers. we've been telling them not to do that but they keep doing it. idk what the right answer is.

- people ask it to remember them across sessions ALL the time. "why doesn't it know I told it yesterday I'm vegetarian." I think marcus told you we'd do memory, just flagging this is a real pain point not a nice-to-have in users' minds

- people want to save recipes they liked. like a favorites list. every single interview.

- one beta tester pasted a 20-page family cookbook PDF into the chat and asked it to "learn her family's recipes." the bot did something weird and she was frustrated. I don't think we need to solve this for v1 but filing it.

- grocery list export came up a bunch. "I planned meals for the week, now give me a shopping list." probably v2 but wanted to flag.

- oh also - a few users have asked if the bot can help them when they're actively cooking, like hands-free. might be worth thinking about

one more thing: beta users tell us they WANT it to have personality. the ones who love it describe it like a friend. the ones who don't describe it like "another chatbot." so marcus isn't wrong about that part, even if he's maximalist about everything else.

oh and heads up, the team is a little rattled after Dana left. especially the platform folks. just so you know the vibe going in.
